Selected academic works

Symplectic geometry: study of infinite staircases of ellipsoid embedding capacity functions.

Farley, C., Holm, T., Magill, N., Schroder, J., Weiler, M., Wang, Z., & Zabelina, E. (2022). Four-periodic infinite staircases for four-dimensional polydisks (arXiv:2210.15069). arXiv.

Project completed under supervision of Dr. Weiler and Dr. Tara Holm (Cornell Unversity) as a part of a summer research program.

Symplectic four-dimensional manifold can or cannot be embedded in a four-dimensional ellipsoid of a given size (eccentricity). The ellipsoid embedding function of a given symplectic four-dimensional manifold takes as an argument the eccentricity of the ellipsoid and outputs a number by which the manifold should be scaled in order to admit an embedding of that ellipsoid. In some cases, this function "looks like" a staircase, and in more unique cases, the number of "steps" is infinite, and they accumulate towards a point. In this research project, we studied ellipsoid embedding function of a polydisk (a toric domain). The goal was to find a new infinite staircase. Using different theorems (tools) for constructing embeddings and implementing the algorithms in Wolfram Mathematica and Python, we found a polydisk for which the ellipsoid embedding function is an infinite staircase.

Information science, dynamical systems, and geometry in the study of neural representations.

This paper introduces concepts from information science such as Shannon complexity, Kolmogorov complexity, and optimal specification method into study of neural representation. Further, I mathematically define a framework of brain as a dynamical system in which representations are presented as trajectories. Finally, I discuss geometry of the state space referring to Voronoi tessellation, the concept of path homotopies and the concept of a fundamental group of a topological space. This research will be continued in the future semesters.

Defining the inside and the outside in the human experience: bodily boundaries, ownership, and awareness.

In this work, I begin with outlining Jacques Derrida's deconstruction method applied to binary terms "inside/outside". Then, I use psychological evidence to investigate the notion of boundary between human body's inside and the outside. I claim that the boudary is not well-defined, and is sometimes extended and redefined. Investigating perception of this boundary is an interesting direction in psychology of interoception, offline and online representations, and assimilation.
